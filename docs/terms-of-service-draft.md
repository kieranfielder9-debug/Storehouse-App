# Storehouse Terms of Service

**Last updated:** [date] · **Draft version**

Thanks for considering Storehouse. Before you use it, please read this — it explains what Storehouse actually is (and isn't), what we ask of you, and what you can expect from us. We've tried to write it the way we'd explain it to a friend, not the way a generic app would.

By creating an account, you agree to these terms and to our [Privacy Notice](/privacy-notice-draft.md).

## 1. What Storehouse is

Storehouse is a **personal financial stewardship tool**. It helps you:

- Track your own income and spending by hand (the "ledger"), and mark things as needs or wants.
- Set stewardship goals — a tithe percentage and a generosity target — that are yours to define.
- Keep a private reflections journal, if you choose to turn that feature on (see our Privacy Notice, Section 6, for how that consent works).
- Extend a small set of features to your own household — for example, letting a parent/guardian track and approve rewards for their own child through the "Wisdom Wallet" feature.

Storehouse is designed around a simple rule: **real functionality is only ever extended to the account owner and their own household members** (people you'd genuinely call family — not friends, not other users, not the public). Anything that would mean holding or moving money for people outside your own household waits until we have the regulatory authorisation the law requires for that — see Section 3.

## 2. What Storehouse is not (yet)

We'd rather tell you plainly what Storehouse doesn't do than let you assume:

- **Storehouse is not a bank.** We do not hold your money. Nothing in the app moves real currency into or out of an account on your behalf today.
- **Storehouse is not a payment service.** We don't initiate payments, transfers, or card transactions.
- **Storehouse is not a regulated investment platform.** Anything you see in an "Invest" or "Kingdom Capital" tab is a **simulation** — illustrative numbers to help you think about stewardship and investing concepts, not real securities, real returns, or real equity crowdfunding. No real money is invested, held, or at risk through these features. We will say so clearly, in the app itself, wherever these features appear, for as long as that remains true.
- **Bank account connection (Plaid) is not yet active for real users.** Storehouse has groundwork in place to eventually support real, automated bank-transaction syncing. As of this version, any "connected bank" data you see is sandbox/simulated data, not a live connection to your real bank account. We'll tell you clearly, and ask for fresh consent, before that changes.

If any of the above changes — for example, if we obtain the regulatory permissions needed to move real money — we'll update these terms and tell you directly, not just edit this page quietly.

## 3. The regulatory line, plainly

UK financial services law draws a hard line at the point money is moved or held for someone other than the account owner or their own household. Storehouse today sits entirely on the free side of that line: everything is either your own manually-entered data, your own bank data (once connected), or record-keeping for your own literal family members. We take that line seriously and won't cross it without the authorisation the law requires — full FCA authorisation, a registered lighter-touch permission, or a licensed banking partner, depending on the feature. Until then, "real money" in Storehouse means *your* real money, tracked, not moved.

- You're responsible for keeping your login credentials secure and for what happens under your account.
- Give us accurate information when you sign up. If your email changes, please keep it updated so we can reach you.

## 5. Acceptable use

Use Storehouse for what it's for: managing your own household's finances honestly. In plain terms, please don't:

- Use Storehouse to store or track data for anyone who isn't genuinely part of your own household (this isn't a tool for managing other people's money, even informally).
- Try to access another user's data, probe the app for vulnerabilities beyond responsible disclosure, or attempt to circumvent the access controls that keep every user's data private to them.
- Use the service for anything unlawful, fraudulent, or intended to mislead — including entering false household member records to obtain features not meant for you.
- Scrape, resell, or redistribute the app or its content without our written permission.

We may suspend or terminate an account that breaches this section, with notice where reasonably possible.

## 6. Your data and content

You own the content you put into Storehouse — your ledger entries, your reflections, your goals. We store it to provide the service to you, as described in our Privacy Notice, and we don't claim ownership over it or use it for anything beyond running the app and improving it in aggregate/anonymised form.

## 7. Deleting your account

You can delete your Storehouse account at any time from Settings, or by contacting **[contact email]**. Deleting your account removes your personal data as described in our Privacy Notice (Section 4) — your ledger, reflections, goals, and household member records. This is a genuine deletion, not a soft-hide: we're not interested in holding data we no longer have a reason to keep.

We may terminate or suspend your account if you breach Section 5, if we're required to by law, or if we discontinue the service — in the latter case, we'll give you reasonable notice and a chance to export your data first, wherever practicable.
